# Privacy Policy

**Tabby — Grouped Window Switcher**
Last updated: 17 June 2026

## The short version

**Tabby does not collect, store, transmit, sell, or share any of your data. Ever.**

There are no analytics, no telemetry, no tracking, no accounts, no servers, and no network connections. Nothing about you or your activity ever leaves your Mac.

## What Tabby reads, and why

To do its job — letting you switch between windows and browser tabs — Tabby reads the following **locally, in memory, only while it is running**:

- **Window titles and application names** (via macOS Accessibility) — to show you the list of open windows.
- **Browser tab titles and URLs** (via macOS Automation / Apple Events) — to show and switch between your open tabs.
- **Window images** (via macOS Screen Recording) — to render live preview thumbnails of the selected window.

This information is used **exclusively** to display the switcher on your screen. It is held in memory only, discarded when no longer needed, and **never written to disk, never transmitted off your device, and never given to any third party**.

## What Tabby stores

The only thing Tabby saves is your **preferences** — your chosen keyboard shortcut, dock visibility, and launch-at-login setting — stored locally in macOS `UserDefaults` on your own Mac. These contain no personal data and never leave your device.

## GDPR & data protection

Because Tabby performs all processing locally and **does not collect or transmit any personal data**, there is no data controller or processor handling your information on remote systems, and there is nothing for us to access, export, or delete on your behalf.

Under the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and similar laws (e.g. UK GDPR, CCPA), you retain full rights over your data — and in Tabby's case those rights are honored by design: the data simply never leaves your control. Uninstalling the app removes its locally stored preferences.

## Permissions you can revoke at any time

You may revoke any macOS permission (Accessibility, Automation, Screen Recording) at any time via **System Settings → Privacy & Security**. Doing so will disable the related feature but will not otherwise affect your data, because none was ever stored or sent.

## Changes to this policy

If this policy ever changes, the "Last updated" date above will be revised. Since Tabby collects nothing, any change will only ever clarify — never expand — what is described here.
